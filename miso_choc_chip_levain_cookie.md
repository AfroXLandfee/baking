# Brown Butter Dark Chocolate Miso Cookies

> **Prep time:** 25 minutes | **Chill time:** 1 hour 10 minutes | **Bake time:** 10-13 minutes
> **Bake Temp:** 425°F (220°C)

---

## Ingredients

* **280 g** (1.25 cups) brown butter, *slightly cooled*
* **450 g** (1 lb) dark chocolate (about 60-70% cacao), *roughly chopped*
* **230 g** (1.5 cups) cake flour
* **275 g** (2 cups) all-purpose flour
* **5 g** (1.5 tsp) kosher salt
* **8 g** (2 tsp) cornstarch
* **6 g** (½ tsp) baking soda
* **285 g** (1.25 cups) light brown sugar
* **115 g** (½ cup) granulated sugar
* **2** large eggs
* **3** large egg yolks
* **40 g** dark miso

---

## Instructions

### Step 1: Prep Dry Ingredients
1.  In a medium bowl, **whisk** together the cake flour, all-purpose flour, kosher salt, cornstarch, and baking soda. Set aside.

### Step 2: Mix the Wet Ingredients
1.  Using a stand mixer fitted with the whisk attachment, **add** the light brown sugar, granulated sugar, and the slightly cooled brown butter.
2.  **Mix** on medium speed for **3 minutes**.
3.  **Stop mixing and wait** for **5-10 minutes** to let the mixture rest.
4.  **Mix on high speed** until the sugar is fully dissolved and the mixture is very creamy.
5.  **Add** the dark miso, then add the eggs one at a time followed by the egg yolks. Make sure to mix each fully into the batter before adding the next.

### Step 3: Combine and Chill
1.  Once smooth, switch to the paddle attachment and **mix in** the dry flour mixture until just smooth.
2.  Turn the machine off and **fold in** the chopped dark chocolate until combined.
3.  Place the batter in a bowl, cover, and **refrigerate** for at least **45 minutes** to chill completely.

### Step 4: Portion and Bake
1.  **Preheat** the oven to **425°F (220°C)**.
2.  **Divide** the dough into 6-ounce pieces. Roll each piece into a tightly packed ball; it should be tall. *(Tip: Use a Vollrath 4-ounce stainless steel disher to form the cookies, making sure to pack the disher tightly with dough).*
3.  Place 4 cookies on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Repeat with the remaining dough.
4.  For best results, **refrigerate** the portioned cookies again for **25 minutes**.
5.  **Bake** for **10-13 minutes**, or until lightly browned on the outside and slightly underdone on the inside.
6.  Set on a wire rack to **cool slightly** before serving.
